Background

What is momex?

  • Idea after SDSL 2024: why not build a cross-language infrastructure on momepy?
  • Considered wrapping, porting and low-level implementation approaches

  • Two proposals:
    • momer: extending momepy to R users (R wrapper)
    • mome(.rs): cross-language infrastructure for urban morphometrics (low-level implementation in Rust)

momer: extending momepy to R users (wrapper)

  • Why?
    • The potential of momepy reaches beyond Python, and its adoption in the broader scientific community and practice could be significantly enhanced by integrating it with R.
    • It can activate a cross-language morphometrics community in preparation for low-level cross-language infrastructure (see mome(.rs))
  • What?
    • An R package wrapping momepy with reticulate in an sf-compatible way and with synchronised documentation.

mome.rs (or simply mome): cross-language infrastructure for urban morphometrics

  • Why?
    • To enable the broader scientific community and practice to conduct morphological analyses in a scalable way.
    • To provide developers a shared low-level code base that is easily maintainable and serves multiple languages.
  • What?
    • A Rust crate (or more) implementing core urban morphometric functions and algorithms, building on existing infrastructure (e.g., GeoRust, polars), with bindings for momepy, momer, mome.jl, etc.
